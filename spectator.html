ABOUT THE SPECTATOR PROGRAM

Be the eyes of our tournament ecosystem

The Brother's eSports Spectator Program is an initiative designed to maintain fair play and integrity across all our tournaments. As a spectator, you'll be a crucial part of our ecosystem, observing matches, monitoring gameplay, and ensuring rules are followed.

Our spectators play a vital role in upholding the competitive standards that make our tournaments respected in the mobile gaming community. Beyond maintaining fairness, spectators contribute to creating a positive gaming environment where skill and strategy are truly rewarded.

Why Join?

  • Earn competitive payments for each match you spectate
  • Gain insider access to high-level competitive gameplay
  • Develop expertise in competitive gaming rules and strategies
  • Network with players, teams, and other esports professionals
  • Help build a fair and respected competitive gaming community

The program is open to passionate gamers with strong knowledge of mobile esports titles, excellent attention to detail, and a commitment to fairness and integrity. No previous spectating experience is necessary – we provide comprehensive training.

QUALIFICATIONS

What we look for in our spectators

Age Requirement

Must be at least 18 years old to participate in the spectator program.

Gaming Knowledge

Strong understanding of at least two of our tournament games (BGMI, Free Fire, COD Mobile, etc.) including mechanics, strategies, and competitive meta.

Technical Requirements

Stable internet connection (minimum 10 Mbps), a reliable device capable of running the games or spectating tools, and basic screen recording capability.

Time Commitment

Ability to commit to at least 5-10 hours per week, with flexibility for evening and weekend matches when most tournaments take place.

Language Proficiency

Comfortable with English and Hindi communication for reports and team coordination.

Communication Skills

Ability to write clear, concise reports and communicate effectively with tournament administrators and team members.

Fairness and Integrity

Strong ethical standards and commitment to maintaining fair play. Must have no history of bans or penalties in competitive gaming.

HOW TO APPLY

The journey to becoming a Brother's eSports spectator

1

Online Application

Fill out the application form with your personal details, gaming experience, and availability. Be sure to provide accurate information about your gaming knowledge and technical setup.

Estimated time: 15 minutes
2

Initial Screening

Our team reviews applications to ensure basic requirements are met. Selected candidates will receive an email with further instructions within 5-7 days.

Estimated time: 5-7 days
3

Knowledge Test

Complete an online assessment that tests your understanding of games, tournament rules, fair play principles, and basic spectating scenarios.

Estimated time: 30 minutes
4

Interview (if applicable)

Selected candidates may be invited for a brief online interview to discuss their application, experience, and motivations for joining the program.

Estimated time: 20-30 minutes
5

Training Session

Successful applicants will attend online training sessions covering spectating tools, reporting procedures, and tournament operations.

Duration: 2-3 sessions of 1 hour each
6

Probation Period

Complete a 2-week probation period where you'll spectate matches under the guidance of experienced spectators who will provide feedback and support.

Duration: 2 weeks
7

Full Spectator Status

Upon successful completion of the probation period, you'll be granted full spectator status and gain access to the complete range of tournaments and benefits.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the entire application process take?

The complete process typically takes 3-4 weeks from initial application to becoming a full spectator, depending on your availability for training and probation matches.

Can I apply if I don't have previous spectating experience?

Yes! We welcome applicants with no prior spectating experience. As long as you have strong gaming knowledge and meet other requirements, our training program will teach you everything you need to know.

What happens if I fail the knowledge test?

If you don't pass the knowledge test on your first attempt, you'll receive feedback on areas to improve and can apply again after 30 days with additional preparation.

CODE OF CONDUCT

Ethical guidelines for all spectators

Fairness and Impartiality

Spectators must maintain complete neutrality and impartiality when observing matches. Personal biases toward specific players, teams, or gameplay styles must not influence reporting or decision-making.

Confidentiality

Information about tournament strategies, unreleased features, or player communications accessed during spectating must be kept confidential. Sharing such information is strictly prohibited and violates our trust.

Professionalism

Maintain professional conduct at all times when representing Brother's eSports. This includes communication with players, administrators, and other community members, both during matches and in public forums.

Accuracy in Reporting

All reports must be factual, specific, and based on observed evidence. Speculation and unsubstantiated accusations have no place in spectator reporting and can damage the integrity of the program.

Punctuality

Spectators must arrive at assigned matches on time and remain present throughout the entire duration. Reliability is essential for maintaining tournament integrity and schedule.

Continuous Improvement

Commit to ongoing learning and improvement of spectating skills. Embrace feedback, stay updated on game changes, and actively participate in spectator training and development programs.

Respect for Players

Treat all players with respect regardless of their skill level, background, or game performance. Remember that spectators are observers, not critics, and should maintain a supportive community atmosphere.

Consequences of Violations

Breaches of the Code of Conduct may result in:

  • Verbal or written warning
  • Temporary suspension from spectating duties
  • Reduction in spectator tier or earnings
  • Permanent removal from the spectator program
  • In severe cases, platform ban and legal action

Reporting Misconduct

If you witness another spectator violating the Code of Conduct, report it through:

  • The confidential spectator reporting form
  • Direct message to a spectator program administrator
  • Email to spectator-conduct@brothersesports.com

All reports are treated confidentially and investigated thoroughly.
